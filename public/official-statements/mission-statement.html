MISSION STATEMENT AND OPERATING PRINCIPLES
Mission
To advance a peaceful, lawful, and nationally legitimate transition for Iran—toward a secular constitutional order that protects equal citizenship, human dignity, women's full equality, national unity, and peaceful relations with neighbors.
Operating Principles
  • • Legality:
    Advocacy anchored in rule of law, due process, and constitutional constraints on power.
  • • Non-violence:
    Opposition to terrorism and political violence; commitment to peaceful political change through credible public process.
  • • Equal citizenship:
    One civil law for all Iranians—of every faith and none, language, region, and political view.
  • • National unity:
    Iran's territorial integrity is non-negotiable; disputes resolved by courts and ballots, not fragmentation or militia force.
  • • Protection of civilians:
    Priority on preventing harm to civilians, prisoners of conscience, journalists, lawyers, and medical personnel.
  • • Accountability:
    Documentation, evidence preservation, and support for independent investigation; rejection of revenge and collective punishment.
  • • Regional peace:
    A doctrine of non-aggression, respect for borders, and economic cooperation that benefits ordinary families.
Engagement with the International Community
The Office requests that governments and institutions measure all Iran policy by two tests:
(1) does it protect the Iranian people from violence and repression, and (2) does it increase the likelihood of a lawful, nationally legitimate transition without enlarging suffering?
  • Support independent investigations into killings, disappearances, torture, and mass detention.
  • Support access to communications and information (including opposition to blanket internet shutdowns).
  • Apply targeted measures against perpetrators of repression, while avoiding broad measures that collectively punish civilians.
  • Support protection for refugees and diaspora communities subject to intimidation and transnational repression.
